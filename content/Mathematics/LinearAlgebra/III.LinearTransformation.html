III. Linear Transformation¶

1. Introduction¶

a. Definition

  • Let $V$ and $W$ be vector spaces. The function $f: V \rightarrow W$ is called a linear transformation of $V$ into $W$ if the following two properties are true for all $u$ and $v$ in $V$ and for any scalar c
    • $T(u+v) = T(u) + T(v)$
    • $T(cu) = cT(u)$

b. Properties

  • $T(0) = 0$
  • $T(-u) = -T(u)$
  • $T(\sum_{i=1}^{n}c_iu_i) = \sum_{i=1}^{n}T(c_iu_i)$

2. Isomorphism¶

a. Definition

  • A bijective linear map from $V$ to $W$ is called an isomorphism from $V$ to $W$. If there exists an isomorphism between vector spaces $V$ and $W$, they are said to be isomorphic $$V \cong W$$

b. Theorem

  • Let $V$ and $W$ be finite dimensional vector spaces. Then $$V \cong W \Leftrightarrow dim(V)=dim(W)$$
